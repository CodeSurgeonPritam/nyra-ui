Every component library on the market looks the same. Purple gradients. Inter
font. A grid of three cards with rounded corners and a soft shadow. Somewhere
a button has `bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-500 to-purple-500`. You've seen the
website before; you'll see it again tomorrow.

I don't want to ship that website. I don't think most developers do, either —
but the cost of *not* shipping it is high. Either you spend a weekend
reinventing the wheel, or you accept that your work is going to look like
everyone else's. Most people, sensibly, pick the wheel.

Nyra is for the small but stubborn subset who pick the third option:
**components that already don't look like everyone else's.**

## The bet

Three things that go together:

1. **Editorial type.** Instrument Serif italic for display, JetBrains Mono
   for body. Not Inter. Not Geist. The serif sets the tone before any
   component renders.
2. **Electric lime.** `#c8ff3c` against `#0a0a0a`. Not purple. Not blue.
   The accent does the work of a logo before you've added one.
3. **Original motion.** Not "fade in on scroll." Cursor-following spotlights,
   magnetic buttons, drifting auroras, conic-gradient beams. Motion that
   means something, not motion as decoration.

Pick any two of these and you have a decent library. Pick all three and you
have an aesthetic that survives a screenshot.

## What Nyra is not

It's not a design system. There is no `<NyraProvider>`. There are no theme
context providers. There is no opinion about how you structure your routes
or what your CSS-in-JS solution should be.

It's a **catalog**. You open a component, copy the file, paste it in your
project, install the listed dependencies, and you own it. If it breaks, you
fix it — same as any file in your repo. If you don't like the lime, you
override one CSS variable.

This is shadcn's idea, but pointed at a different aesthetic. The components
themselves are different; the *distribution model* isn't.

## Why now

The component library space is having a moment. shadcn cracked the
copy-paste model. Aceternity proved people will pay for motion. Magic UI
proved breadth matters. The next move isn't another shadcn-clone — it's
the library that picks an aesthetic and *commits* to it.

Nyra is that library. Or it will be — you're early.
